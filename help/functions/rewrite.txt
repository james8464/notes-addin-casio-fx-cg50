rewrite(expr,terms)

Args:
  expr, terms

Required:
  expr is the expression or equation to rewrite.
  terms is a list of target expressions.

Optional:
  Target terms may be named with equals signs.
  Example: [a=log(2,x),b=log(2,x+8)]

Does:
  Rewrites the input in terms of the chosen target expressions.
  Uses direct substitution, factorisation, products, quotients, powers, roots, and log laws where useful.

Examples:
  rewrite(log(2,sqrt(x)),[log(2,x),log(2,x+8)])
  rewrite(log(2,x^2+8*x),[a=log(2,x),b=log(2,x+8)])

F2:
  rewrite(log(2,sqrt(x)),[a=log(2,x),b=log(2,x+8)])

F3:
  rewrite(log(2,8+64/x),[a=log(2,x),b=log(2,x+8)])

Keys:
  F1 inserts the command.
  F2 inserts the first example.
  F3 inserts the second example when available.

Working:
  Shows the route used, then ends with only the rewritten expression or equation.

Tip:
  Use named targets when you want a short final result, e.g. a+b instead of log(2,x)+log(2,x+8).
