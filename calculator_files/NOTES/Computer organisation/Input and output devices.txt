# Input and output devices

## BARCODE READERS

### Characteristics

- Optical scanner
  - uses light to read printed barcodes and converts them to digital data
- Types:
  - Pen-type readers
  - Laser scanners
  - CCD (charged-coupled device) readers
  - Camera-based readers
- Accuracy:
  - Very accurate
  - Can quickly capture data, reducing human error
- Portability
  - Portable and can be handheld
  - Convenient for use

### Purposes

- Inventory management
  - retail and warehouses use it for tracking inventory and managing stock levels
- Point of Sale (POS)
  - checkout systems for scanning products and processing sales transactions
- Asset tracking
  - tracking assets and equipment in various industries
  - ensures accurate record-keeping
- Logistics
  - tracks shipments and manages the movement of goods
  - widely used in supply chain management

### Suitability

- Retail
  - quick + accurate scanning of products is essential for efficient checkout and inventory management
- Warehousing
  - streamline inventory tracking
  - reduces errors in stock management
- Healthcare
  - eg tracking medications, patient records, medical equipment, etc

### Principles of Operation

- A light source (ie laser) is shone at barcode
- A moving prism moves light beam across barcode (or the user moves reader across)
- Light is reflected back (ie from the barcode, back to the reader)
  - Black bars absorb more light
  - White spaces reflect more light
- A light sensor detects the reflected light and converts it into an electrical signal
- The electrical signal is processed and translated into binary data via ADC
- The binary data is sent to a connected device (eg a computer) for further processing


## DIGITAL CAMERAS

### Characteristics

- Resolution
  - images are captured in megapixels 
  - higher MP = better quality
  - common resolutions range from 12 MP to over 50 MP
- Storage
  - images are stored on memory cards (eg SD cards)
- LCD screen
  - allows users to preview and review images
- Zoom
  - optical and digital zoom
  - optical zoom - camera's lens used to magnify image
  - digital zoom - image is enlarged digitally
- Shooting modes
  - various shooting modes (eg portrait, landscape, night mode)
  - allows for different styles of photography

### Purposes

- Photography
  - capturing images for personal, professional or artistic purposes
- Videography
  - can record high-definition video
- Documentation
  - can be useful to document events so they can be remembered/referenced
- Content creation
  - eg producing media for social platforms

### Suitability

- Professional use
  - offers advanced features and high resolution that professionals can take advantage of
- Amateur photography
  - anybody can learn photography, starting from nowhere
- Travel/everyday use
  - compact digital cameras are convenient for everyday use + travel

### Principles of Operation

- The camera's lens focuses light onto the sensor
  - The amount of light is controlled by the aperture/diapgragm
  - The duration of exposure is controlled by the shutter speed
- The sensor (eg a CCD) converts the light into electrical signals
  - Each photosite on the sensor corresponds to a pixel in the final image
- The electrical signals are converted to digital data via an ADC
- The digital data is processed by the camera's image processor
  - Adjusts colour, contrast and sharpness
- The processed image is stored on the memory card
- The captured image can be viewed on the camera's LCD screen, and can be transferred from the memory card onto an external supported device

## LASER PRINTERS

### Characteristics

- Print quality
  - high quality printouts
  - excellent resolution and sharpness (better than inkjet printers)
  - ideal for text and graphics
- Speed
  - known for having EXTREMELY fast printing speeds
  - can print multiple pages per minute
  - significantly faster than inkjet printers
- Cost efficiency
  - initial costs can be high (i.e. the printer itself)
  - cost per page is very low (so good for high-volume printing)
- Durability/reliability
  - can handle large print volumes without frequent maintenance
- Noise level
  - operate quietly compared to inkjet printers


### Suitability

- Office printing
  - high speed + efficiency + low cost per page
- High volume printing
  - eg in schools, businesses and print shops
- Professional documents
  - eg brochures, flyers and business cards

### Principles of Operation

- It prints ...
- A laser beam projects an image of the page onto a photosensitive drum coated with a photoconductive material
- The drum is given a uniform electrostatic charge
- The laser beam discharges certain areas of the drum
  - It moves across the drum via a prism (or rotating mirror)
  - Creates an electrostatic image of the page
- Toner (a fine powder) is attracted to the charged areas of the drum
- The toner adheres to the electrostatic image of the drum
- The toner image is transferred from the drum to a sheet of paper
- The paper then passes through a pair of heated rollers
  - The toner is melted onto the paper, creating a permanent image

## RFID

### Characteristics

- Non-contact communication
  - relies on electromagnetic waves
  - the waves communicate between the RFID tag and the reader
  - no need for physical contact
- Speed
  - can read/write data extremely quickly
  - often in less than 100 milliseconds
- Uniqueness
  - each RFID tag has a unique identifier
  - ensures precise identification of objects
- Versatility
  - RFID tags can be passive, active, or semi-passive

### Purposes

- Inventory management
  - widely used for tracking inventory in warehouses and retail stores
  - provides real-time data on stock levels
- Access control
  - used in security systems for controlling access to buildings/restricted areas
- Asset tracking
  - used to track valuable assets (eg equipment, vehicles)
  - ensures their location is always known
- Supply chain management
  - improves efficiency in supply chains
  - automates the tracking of goods from production to delivery

### Suitability

- High-speed identification
  - can rapidly identity many items from a distance
  - useful in logistics and retail
- Automation
  - ideal for automating processes
  - reduces the need for manual intervention, increasing efficiency
- Complex environments
  - can operate effectively in various environments, including those with high-speed moving objects and multiple tags

### Principles of Operation

- RFID tags
  - contains microchip and an antenna
  - microchip stores data
  - antenna transmits and receives signals
- RFID readers
  - emit radio waves that activate RFID tags
  - the tags respond by sending their stored data back to the reader
- Communication
  - reader processes the received data
  - reader can interact with backend databases to update/retrieve information

### Types of Tags

- Passive
  - powered by the reader's signal
  - short range
  - used for applications like inventory tracking
- Active
  - powered by an internal battery
  - longer range than passive tags
  - used for tracking assets over larger distances
- Semi-passive
  - combines features of both active and passive tags
  - uses an internal battery to power microchip
  - still relies on reader's signal to activate
