# Secondary storage devices

## Recap

  - Primary storage:
- Volatile (loses its contents when the computer is switched off)
- E.g., RAM (random access memory)
  - Secondary storage:
- Non-volatile
- Means you can store programs and data for later use
- Have a much larger capacity than primary storage

### Types of Secondary Storage

- Solid state storage devices
  - e.g., USB memory sticks
- Optical storage devices
  - e.g., CD, DVD, and Blu-ray discs
- Magnetic storage devices
  - e.g., hard disk drives

## Characteristics

- Storage capacity
  - very large storage capacities
  - typically ranging from hundreds of GB to several TB
- Speed
  - slower read/write speeds than SSD's
  - avg read/write speeds are around 100/200 MB/s
  - the presence of moving parts (e.g., spinning platters) contributes to slower access times
- Durability
  - susceptible to mechanical failure due to their moving parts
  - sensitive to physical shocks and vibrations
  - likely to damage if dropped
  - essentially since it has moving parts, its not very durable
- Cost
  - more cost effective per GB compared to SSD's
  - it's the better choice if you want lots of storage and don't care about read/write speeds

## Purposes

- Data storage
- Backups (e.g., Time Machine)
- Mass storage

## Suitability

- Cost-effective storage
  - for users who need large storage capacities at a low cost and don't care about slow access speed
  - e.g., for storing backups or virtual machines
- Non performance critical applications
  - some ppl may want to store large applications here rather than on their device

## Principles of Operation

- HDDs consist of one or more spinning platters coated with a magnetic material
- Data is stored on these platters in the form of magnetic patterns
- The read/write heads are positioned on an actuator arm that moves across the platters
- These heads read and write data by detecting and modifying the magnetic patterns on the platters
- The platters are divided into concentric tracks, which are further divided into sectors
- Each sector stores a fixed amount of data
- A motor spins the platters at high speeds, whilst the actuator arm positions the read/write heads over the correct track to access data
- The HDD controller manages:
  - the movement of the actuator arm
  - the spinning of the platters
  - the reading and writing of data
  - error correction and data caching

## Characteristics

- Storage capacity
  - CDs can store up to 700MB
  - DVDs can store up to 4.7GB (single-layer)
  - Blu-ray discs can story up to 25GB (single-layer)
- Durability
  - the discs are relatively durable
  - can last for many years if stored properly
  - susceptible to scratches and environmental damage
- Portability
  - light and portable
  - easy to transport and distribute
- Compatibility
  - widely compatible with various devices that include a suitable reader device

## Purposes

- Media distribution
  - movies, music, software and games
- Data backup
- Software installation
- Multimedia storage
  - i.e. storing photos, videos and audio files

## Suitability

- Good for easy distribution
- Today, it is outdated technology

## Principles of Operation

- A laser beam is focused onto the surface of the disk
- Data is stored in the form of microscopic pits and lands
- The pits and lands represent binary data (0s and 1s)
- The laser beam detects these variations to read the information stored on the disk
- When reading data, the laser beam reflects off the surface of the disk, and the reflected light is detected by a sensor
- When writing data, the laser beam alters the surface of the disk to create the pits and lands
- The optical drive contains:
  - a motor to spin the disk
  - a lens system to focus the laser beam accurately on the data tracks

## Characteristics

- Speed
  - significantly faster read/write speeds than HDD
  - NVMe SSDs can reach up to 5000 MB/s
- Reliability
  - no moving parts = more durable + less prone to mechanical failure
- Energy efficiency
  - consumes less power than HDDs
    - leads to longer battery life in laptops and lower energy costs in data centres
- Size and weight
  - smaller + lighter than HDDs
  - ideal for portable devices (e.g., laptops and tablets)
- Noise
  - operate completely silently (since there are no moving parts)
  - can get hot though, so if you buy a separate enclosure with it, the enclosure may have some fans

## Purposes

- OS drives
  - commonly used as the primary drive for OS due to fast boot times and quick access to system files
- Application loading
  - applications installed on SSDs load faster
  - improves overall system performance
- Gaming
  - reduces load times for games
- Data storage
  - used for storing frequently accessed data due to high speed access

## Suitability

- Performance-critical applications
  - suitable for applications that require fast data access and high performance
  - e.g., video editing, gaming, and software development
- Portable devices
  - due to their small size, light weight and good durability
- Enterprise use
  - used in data centres and servers where high-speed data access and reliability are crucial

## Principles of Operation

- NAND flash memory cells are used to store data
- These cells are made of floating-gate transistors that can retain an electrical charge
- When data is written, electrons are forced into the floating gates of the transistors, changing their charge state
- This change is used to represent binary data
- When data is read from an SSD, the charge state of the transistors is checked to determine the stored binary data
- SSDs have a controller that manages:
  - data storage and retrieval
  - wear levelling
  - error correction

| Storage Type | Capacity | Speed of Access | Suitability |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| HDD | Large storage capacities (hundreds of GB to several TB) | Slower read and write speeds (100-200 MB/s) | Cost-effective for large storage needs, slower performance, suitable for archival storage |
| Optical Disks (CDs, DVDs, Blu-ray) | Lower storage capacities (up to 700 MB for CDs, up to 4.7 GB for DVDs, up to 25 GB for Blu-ray) | Slower read and write speeds, significantly slower access times than HDDs and SSDs | Distributing media, software, long-term archival storage where data integrity is important |
| SSDs | Various capacities (128 GB to several TB) | Significantly faster read and write speeds (500-550 MB/s for SATA SSDs, up to 3500-5000 MB/s for NVMe SSDs) | High-performance tasks, operating system drives, application loading, gaming, portable devices |
