# Analogue digital conversion

## How Does ADC Work?

- ADC takes samples of the analogue data at regular intervals
  - The frequency that the samples are taken is measured in Hz
  - Higher frequency = better reproduction of the analogue signal
- The samples are quantised (the amplitude of each sample is approximated to an integer value)
- Each sample is assigned/encoded to a binary value

## How Does Dac Work?

- The DAC takes digital binary data as input
- Each binary value is converted into a corresponding analogue voltage level
- The voltages are output in sequence to reconstruct the signal
- The signal is smoothed (e.g. using a low-pass filter) to produce a continuous analogue waveform

- Many sensors (eg temperature sensors and microphones) output an analogue signal.
- An ADC would be required here so that the computer can actually make use of the data.

- The most common use for a DAC is to convert a digital audio signal to an analogue signal
