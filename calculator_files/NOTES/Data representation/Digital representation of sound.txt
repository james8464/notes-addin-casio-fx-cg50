# Digital representation of sound

Sound is represented as a sequence of samples

## Sample Resolution

- Number of bits allocated to store each sample
- Higher sample resolution = greater audio quality + higher file size

## Sampling Rate

- Number of samples per second
- Measured in Hz (Hertz)

## Nyquist Theorem

- states that the sampling rate of a digital audio file should be at least TWICE the frequency of the sound
- Sound might not be faithfully represented if its less than this


### Formula to Calculate File Size (of Sound File)

  - File size = Sample resolution * sample rate * duration (in seconds)
  - However, this value is a minimum. In reality, it will be higher due to metadata
