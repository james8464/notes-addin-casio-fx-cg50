# large data set

## what it is

- Edexcel paper 3 can test familiarity with the LDS
- the full LDS is not given in the exam
- if calculations are needed, the question gives the needed extract
- key advantage is knowing variables, units, context, missing data and likely interpretations



## time periods

- weather data from May to October
- years are 1987 and 2015
- each May to October period has 184 days
- May has 31 days
- June has 30 days
- July has 31 days
- August has 31 days
- September has 30 days
- October has 31 days
- 1987 can be compared with 2015
- October 1987 includes the Great Storm, so wind and gust data can be unusual



## UK locations

### south to north

- Camborne
- Hurn
- Heathrow
- Leeming
- Leuchars

### location facts

- Camborne is in Cornwall, south west, coastal, often windier
- Hurn is in Dorset, south coast area
- Heathrow is in Greater London, inland, airport station
- Leeming is in North Yorkshire, inland
- Leuchars is in Scotland, coastal, often windier and cooler

### useful comparisons

- further north generally means cooler during May to October
- coastal locations may be windier
- southern locations are usually warmer
- sunshine and temperature often have positive association
- rainfall is variable and not normally distributed



## overseas locations

- Beijing is in China, northern hemisphere
- Jacksonville is in Florida, USA, hot and humid
- Perth is in Western Australia, southern hemisphere

### key context

- Perth has reversed seasons compared with the UK
- May to October is autumn, winter and spring for Perth, not summer
- Beijing can be hot and wetter in summer
- Jacksonville is generally warm and humid
- overseas sheets contain fewer variables than UK sheets



## UK variables

### daily mean temperature

- unit is deg C
- recorded from 0900 to 0900
- usually given to 1 decimal place
- typical range is roughly 5 to 24 deg C

### daily total rainfall

- unit is mm
- recorded over 0900 to 0900
- many values are 0 or tr
- rainfall is usually skewed, so normal models may be poor

### daily total sunshine

- unit is hours
- recorded over 0000 to 2400
- usually between 0 and about 14 hours
- more sunshine is expected in summer months

### daily mean windspeed

- unit is knots
- recorded over 0000 to 2400
- also given as a Beaufort conversion
- Beaufort values are qualitative, such as calm, light, moderate

### daily maximum gust

- unit is knots
- maximum instantaneous wind speed over the day
- can be much higher than daily mean windspeed

### daily maximum relative humidity

- unit is percent
- integer values
- above 95 percent is associated with mist or fog

### daily mean total cloud

- unit is oktas
- oktas measure eighths of the sky covered by cloud
- discrete integer values from 0 to 8

### daily mean visibility

- unit is Dm
- 1 Dm = 10 m
- measures horizontal visibility
- often rounded to the nearest 100 Dm

### daily mean pressure

- unit is hPa
- 1 hPa = 100 Pa = 1 millibar
- typical range is about 990 to 1040 hPa

### wind direction

- given in degrees and as a cardinal direction
- direction means where the wind comes from
- UK data includes mean wind direction and gust direction



## overseas variables

### missing compared with UK

- no sunshine

## cleaning data

### trace rainfall

- tr means trace rainfall
- it means rainfall greater than 0 but less than 0.05 mm
- in calculations, tr may be cleaned as 0 mm if treating trace as negligible
- in calculations, tr may be cleaned as 0.025 mm if using a midpoint estimate
- always follow the wording in the question

### missing data

- n/a means data is not available
- missing data should normally be removed before calculations
- do not treat n/a as 0
- some early May 1987 UK values are missing for sunshine, windspeed and gust



## variable types

- temperature is continuous
- rainfall is continuous, but has many 0 and tr values
- sunshine is continuous
- windspeed is continuous, but recorded rounded
- Beaufort conversion is qualitative ordinal
- humidity percent is quantitative discrete after rounding
- cloud cover is discrete, 0 to 8
- visibility is quantitative and heavily rounded
- pressure is quantitative
- cardinal direction is qualitative



## common exam points

### sampling

- random samples should be selected after cleaning data
- systematic sample means choose a random start, then take every kth value
- stratified sample can be by month, year or location
- if comparing years, check both samples use equivalent months and locations

### diagrams and statistics

- box plots compare median, spread, skew and outliers
- histograms are useful for continuous grouped data with unequal class widths
- scatter diagrams test association between two variables
- rainfall often has many zeros, so mean can be affected by extreme values
- cloud cover is discrete, so a normal model is usually unsuitable

### correlation and regression

- correlation does not prove causation
- interpolation is usually safer than extrapolation
- regression should only be used in the range and context of the data
- be careful comparing data from different years, months or locations

### hypothesis tests

- LDS data can supply sample sizes, proportions, means or correlations
- state hypotheses clearly
- use the correct distribution or test statistic
- give a conclusion in context
